About

Always Iterating. Always Improving.

Great design involves iterating and improving, and my journey to UX design has followed a similar approach. From marketing to project management, all of my roles have had the same passions connecting them: a need to understand how humans work and a desire to solve their problems.

In marketing, I used market research to see existing services through the lens of consumers and determine which of their problems our services could solve. Whether it was through a poster leading a struggling student to counseling services or an email inviting a local food influencer to join our online community, I worked to connect consumers to the solutions they needed.

As a project manager, I collaborated with billing offices in hospitals across the country to streamline workflows and simplify the billing experience for end users and patients. While I enjoyed my previous roles, my marketing work did not bring me the joy that came from directly designing these hospitalsâ€™ solutions.

With this in mind, I began my transition to UX design by taking a design role in web publishing. Through transforming technical data into digestible web pages, I developed my knowledge of Gestalt principles and honed my user interface design skills. To finish the UX transition, I completed the CareerFoundry UX Design bootcamp where I combined all of my research and design skills to create the Expert web app.

My journey may have its twists, but those twists have made me dynamic and adaptable. Using my diverse experience, I am able to bridge the gap between business goals and customer needs, always working to ensure that no story goes unheard and no problem goes unsolved.
